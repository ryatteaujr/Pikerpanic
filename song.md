# Warehouse Picker Panic Music Prompts

## Track 1: Expedite The Load

Create a 30-second loopable retro arcade warehouse game track called "Expedite The Load." The mood is energetic but controlled, like the first shift just started and the picker truck is rolling with confidence. Use punchy 16-bit drums, tight FM bass, bright chiptune lead, muted industrial percussion, and subtle forklift backup-beep motifs tucked into the rhythm. Tempo around 132 BPM. Keep the melody catchy and heroic, with a clean four-bar hook that can loop without feeling abrupt. The intensity should be medium: focused, upbeat, and playful, not chaotic yet. No vocals. End with a clean turnaround that loops back naturally.

## Track 2: Schedule Failure Imminent

Create a 30-second loopable retro arcade warehouse game track called "Schedule Failure Imminent." This should build directly from the feel of "Expedite The Load," but make the shift more urgent. Use the same chiptune palette, but add faster hi-hats, sharper snare hits, warning-siren synth stabs, busier bass movement, and a second counter-melody that feels like warehouse alarms and scanner pings. Tempo around 146 BPM. The track should feel like the truck timer is dropping, the chute moved, and the forklift traffic is getting risky. Keep it fun and arcade-like, not dark horror. No vocals. End with a rising phrase that can loop smoothly.

## Track 3: Priority Override

Create a 30-second loopable retro arcade warehouse game track called "Priority Override." This is the most intense cue and should feel like level two is in full panic: more picks, fragile freight, water heater load, moved chute, and no room for mistakes. Build on the motifs from the first two tracks, but push everything harder: driving 16-bit breakbeat drums, aggressive synth bass, rapid arpeggios, alarm-like brass synth hits, metallic warehouse percussion, and a triumphant but frantic lead melody. Tempo around 160 BPM. Make it exciting, high-score focused, and slightly unhinged while still clean and playable under game sound effects. No vocals. End with a tight loop point that snaps back into the opening beat.
