# Hard Goods Mezzanine Music Prompts

## Level 4: Hard Goods Climb

Create a 30-second loopable retro arcade music track called "Hard Goods Climb" for a Donkey Kong-style warehouse mezzanine platform level. The player is on foot, climbing ladders and ramps through blue steel platforms, yellow safety rails, stacked boxes, tools, paint cans, plumbing bins, and hard goods storage. Use 16-bit arcade drums, bouncy FM bass, bright chiptune lead, metallic warehouse percussion, and a playful ladder-climb melodic hook. Tempo around 138 BPM. The mood should feel adventurous and vertical: new level, new rules, warehouse danger, but still fun and readable. Add subtle rolling-box percussion accents, but keep the intensity controlled. No vocals. End with a clean loop point that returns naturally to the opening beat.

## Level 5: Ramp Runaway

Create a 30-second loopable retro arcade music track called "Ramp Runaway" that builds on "Hard Goods Climb" but increases urgency. This is a warehouse mezzanine platform level with steeper ramps, more falling freight, narrow platforms, workers in safety zones, and more aggressive movement. Use the same 16-bit arcade palette, but add faster hi-hats, sharper snare, rolling tom fills that mimic boxes bouncing down ramps, alarm-like synth stabs, and a busier bassline. Tempo around 152 BPM. The melody should feel like the player is weaving through ladders and ramps while heavy boxes tumble nearby. Energetic, tense, and high-score focused, not horror. No vocals. End with a rising loop turnaround.

## Level 6: Mezzanine Mayhem

Create a 30-second loopable retro arcade music track called "Mezzanine Mayhem" as the most intense hard-goods mezzanine cue. It should feel like a final arcade platform gauntlet: multiple ladders, long ramps, falling water heaters, rolling boxes, workers to avoid, tight jumps, and a boss/operator dropping freight from the top platform. Build on motifs from the previous two mezzanine songs, but push everything harder: rapid arpeggios, aggressive synth bass, 16-bit breakbeat drums, metallic clang percussion, warning-beep accents, and a frantic heroic lead melody. Tempo around 164 BPM. Make it chaotic but still clean enough to sit under game sound effects. No vocals. End with a tight loop point that snaps directly back into the first beat.
