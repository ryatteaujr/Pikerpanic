# Grand Finale Songs

Three 30-second tracks for Levels 7-9. Each one should loop cleanly, build intensity from the previous track, and keep the same warehouse-panic identity: fast picking, truck deadlines, machinery, scanners, dock alarms, and heroic last-minute focus.

## Level 7: Conveyor Belt Sort

**Title:** Sort Line Surge

**Prompt:** Create a 30-second arcade warehouse action track for a conveyor sorting level. Start with tight percussion like rollers clicking in rhythm, add bright synth pulses like barcode scanners, and keep a steady forward push. The mood should feel urgent but controlled: the player is learning the outbound line, dodging belt traffic, and racing to sort boxes before they reach the wrong chute. Use punchy drums, short bass stabs, mechanical clacks, and a confident hook that loops cleanly.

## Level 8: Loading Dock Rush

**Title:** Dock Door Deadline

**Prompt:** Create a 30-second high-energy loading dock track that builds on the conveyor theme with heavier drums, louder bass, and industrial warning tones. The feeling is outside at the dock: truck doors opening, pallet jacks rolling, forklifts crossing, and bay lights changing. Add rising synth sirens, metallic impacts, and a driving bassline that feels like the truck is almost ready to leave. It should be more intense than Level 7 but still crisp and playable.

## Level 9: Final Truck Load Panic

**Title:** Last Truck Standing

**Prompt:** Create a 30-second final-level boss-rush warehouse track. Combine conveyor rhythm, dock alarms, forklift danger, and heroic arcade melody into one urgent finale. Start immediately with full momentum, then layer rapid drums, pulsing bass, warning beeps, and a bright triumphant lead. The mood is controlled chaos: final truck loading, changing drop-off target, fragile freight, workers nearby, and seconds left on the clock. It should feel like the biggest level in the game and loop seamlessly without losing intensity.
